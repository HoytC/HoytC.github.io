Origin Story

Super Smash Bros. was first released for the Nintendo 64 game console in 1999. Its creator, Masahiro Sakurai was previously best known as the creator of the Kirby video game series that included many titles such as Kirby's Dream Land and Kirby Super Star for the Super Nintendo game console. Smash Bros is a crossover game featuring many of Nintendo's most beloved video game characters who duke it for supremacy in zany multi-player battles. The original cast featured twelve total characters:

Mario, Luigi, Donkey Kong, Link, Samus, Yoshi, Kirby, Fox, Pikachu, Captain Falcon, Ness and Jigglypuff.

Later installments added a plethora of new characters from both first and second-party Nintendo titles and were released for the Nintendo Gamecube as Super Smash Bros. Melee, for the Nintendo Wii as Super Smash Bros. Brawl, for the Nintendo Wii-U and 3DS as Super Smash Bros. 4, and most recently for the Nintendo Switch as Super Smash Bros. Ultimate. Each game has become one of Nintendo's best-selling games for its respective console generation and has been praised for its unique take on the fighting game formula, fun party mechanics and large cast of characters that present a wide range of appeal to gamers and non-gamers alike.
